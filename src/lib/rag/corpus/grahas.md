# The Nine Grahas

Jyotiṣa reads nine grahas: the seven visible bodies plus the two lunar nodes. A graha is not merely a planet but a "grasper" — an agent that takes hold of a portion of experience and delivers it at its appointed time.

## Sun (Sūrya)

The Sun is the ātmakāraka in the natural sense: the soul, vitality, the father, authority, government, bone and constitution, the right eye in a male chart. He signifies self-expression, dignity, ambition and the capacity to command.

The Sun is exalted in Aries, debilitated in Libra, and owns Leo. He is a natural malefic by his heat and separative quality, though never cruel in the way Saturn or Mars can be. Planets within about eight degrees of the Sun become combust (astaṅgata) and lose their power to deliver results independently — Venus and Mercury tolerate combustion far better than Mars, Jupiter or Saturn.

A strong Sun gives confidence, a straight spine, recognition from authority and a settled relationship with the father. A weak or afflicted Sun gives difficulty with authority figures, low vitality, problems with the eyes, heart or bones, and a brittle rather than settled pride.

## Moon (Candra)

The Moon is the mind — manas — and the mother, the emotional body, memory, the public, liquids, and comfort. In Vedic astrology the Moon carries far more weight than in Western practice: the birth nakshatra is reckoned from the Moon, the entire Vimśottari daśā is calculated from her position, and transits are judged primarily from the Moon rather than the ascendant.

The Moon is exalted in Taurus, debilitated in Scorpio, and owns Cancer. She is a benefic when waxing and bright, and progressively less so as she darkens; a Moon within a few degrees of the Sun (amāvasyā, the dark Moon) is considered weak and gives an unsettled mind.

A strong Moon gives emotional steadiness, popularity, a nourishing mother and an imaginative inner life. An afflicted Moon gives anxiety, sleeplessness, mood that swings with circumstance, and difficulty feeling at home anywhere.

## Mars (Maṅgala, Kuja)

Mars is energy, courage, conflict, brothers, land and property, engineering, surgery, muscle and blood. He is the commander — direct, hot, impatient, willing to break what obstructs him.

Mars is exalted in Capricorn, debilitated in Cancer, and owns Aries and Scorpio. He aspects the 4th and 8th houses from himself in addition to the 7th, which is why his placement disturbs domestic peace and longevity matters so readily.

A well-placed Mars gives decisiveness, physical courage, technical skill and the ability to finish what is started. An afflicted Mars gives anger, accidents, cuts and burns, litigation, and friction with siblings. Mars in the 1st, 2nd, 4th, 7th, 8th or 12th from the ascendant forms Maṅgal doṣa, discussed under afflictions.

## Mercury (Budha)

Mercury is intellect, speech, commerce, calculation, writing, wit, skin and the nervous system. He is the youngest of the grahas and takes on the colouring of whatever he sits with — a quality classical texts call his neutrality.

Mercury is exalted in Virgo (uniquely, he is both exalted and in his own sign there), debilitated in Pisces, and owns Gemini and Virgo. He is a natural benefic when unafflicted and becomes malefic in the company of malefics.

A strong Mercury gives quick comprehension, persuasive speech, aptitude for trade, mathematics and language. An afflicted Mercury gives scattered thinking, nervous complaints, speech defects, and a tendency to cleverness without judgement.

## Jupiter (Guru, Bṛhaspati)

Jupiter is wisdom, dharma, the teacher, children, wealth in its expansive sense, the liver, and grace. He is the greatest natural benefic; his aspect on any house or planet protects it.

Jupiter is exalted in Cancer, debilitated in Capricorn, and owns Sagittarius and Pisces. He aspects the 5th and 9th houses from himself in addition to the 7th — a wide, generous reach that is why Jupiter's aspect on the ascendant or the Moon is so protective.

A strong Jupiter gives optimism, ethical clarity, good counsel, teachers who appear at the right moment, and children. An afflicted or debilitated Jupiter gives excess, complacency, misplaced faith, weight and liver trouble, and difficulty with progeny or with guidance.

## Venus (Śukra)

Venus is love, beauty, art, comfort, vehicles, the spouse (particularly in a male chart), reproductive fluids, and refinement. He is the teacher of the asuras and governs the whole domain of enjoyment.

Venus is exalted in Pisces, debilitated in Virgo, and owns Taurus and Libra. He is a natural benefic and the strongest karaka for marriage.

A strong Venus gives charm, artistic capacity, a comfortable married life, and an eye for quality. An afflicted Venus gives indulgence, difficulty in relationship, diabetes and kidney or reproductive complaints, and a taste that outruns the means to satisfy it.

## Saturn (Śani)

Saturn is time, discipline, endurance, labour, old age, servants, sorrow, and the slow ripening of karma. He is the great teacher by deprivation: what Saturn takes, he takes in order to instruct.

Saturn is exalted in Libra, debilitated in Aries, and owns Capricorn and Aquarius. He aspects the 3rd and 10th houses from himself in addition to the 7th. He is the strongest natural malefic, but as lord of a trine for certain ascendants — notably Taurus and Libra — he becomes a yogakāraka and gives outstanding results.

A well-placed Saturn gives patience, structure, the capacity to work long without reward, longevity and eventual authority earned rather than given. An afflicted Saturn gives delay, chronic complaints, isolation, poverty of spirit if not of means, and the sense that everything must be paid for twice.

## Rāhu and Ketu — the lunar nodes

Rāhu and Ketu are not physical bodies but the two points where the Moon's path crosses the ecliptic. They are always exactly opposite each other and always retrograde. They have no ownership of signs in the classical scheme and are said to give the results of their dispositor and of any planet they conjoin.

Rāhu is desire, obsession, foreignness, technology, illusion, sudden and unearned elevation, and the hunger that cannot be satisfied by its object. He magnifies whatever he touches. Rāhu in a favourable house can give enormous worldly success, typically unconventional in its route.

Ketu is detachment, moksha, past-life mastery, sudden loss, insight without words, and the surrender of what Rāhu grasps at. Ketu gives skill so ingrained it feels effortless, alongside a persistent dissatisfaction with the very field in which it operates. Ketu on a planet or house tends to first remove and then refine.

## Natural benefics and malefics

Natural benefics: Jupiter, Venus, waxing Moon, and unafflicted Mercury.
Natural malefics: Saturn, Mars, Sun, Rāhu, Ketu, waning Moon, and Mercury in malefic company.

This natural character is only half the story. Functional character — whether a graha rules good houses from the ascendant — frequently overrides it. Saturn is a natural malefic but a functional yogakāraka for a Taurus or Libra ascendant. Jupiter is the greatest natural benefic but a functional difficulty for a Taurus or Virgo ascendant, where he rules two difficult houses.
