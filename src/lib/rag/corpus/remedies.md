# Remedial Measures (Upāya)

Remedies in Jyotiṣa are not a way of overriding a chart. They are a way of strengthening what is weak, moderating what is excessive, and — most importantly — bringing conscious attention to a pattern that would otherwise run unexamined.

The traditional order of efficacy places conduct first and objects last: right action, then charity, then mantra and ritual, and only then gemstones.

## Principles

**Strengthen a weak benefic; do not strengthen a strong malefic.** The commonest error is wearing a stone for an afflicting planet, which amplifies the affliction. A gemstone should be chosen for a planet that is functionally benefic for the ascendant and weak — not for a planet that is already dominant and causing trouble.

**Match the remedy to the function, not the natural character.** Saturn is a natural malefic but a yogakāraka for Taurus and Libra ascendants; blue sapphire may be excellent for them and disastrous elsewhere. Jupiter is the great benefic but a functional difficulty for Taurus and Virgo ascendants.

**Charity is given to the planet you wish to pacify.** Gemstones strengthen; dāna placates. When a malefic is causing difficulty, give away its substances rather than wearing them.

## By graha

**Sun** — Strengthen with ruby (māṇikya), copper, and offering water to the rising Sun. Colours: gold, orange, deep red. Day: Sunday. Mantra: Oṁ Sūryāya Namaḥ, or the Āditya Hṛdayam. Charity to pacify: wheat, jaggery, copper, on a Sunday.

**Moon** — Pearl (muktā) or moonstone, silver. Colours: white, silver, pale cream. Day: Monday. Mantra: Oṁ Candrāya Namaḥ. Charity: rice, milk, white cloth, silver. Serving one's mother is the strongest lunar remedy.

**Mars** — Red coral (mūṅgā). Colours: red, coral. Day: Tuesday. Mantra: Oṁ Aṅgārakāya Namaḥ, or the Hanumān Chālīsā, which is the most widely used Mars remedy in practice. Charity: red lentils, copper. Physical exercise and disciplined exertion are genuine Mars remedies.

**Mercury** — Emerald (pannā). Colours: green. Day: Wednesday. Mantra: Oṁ Budhāya Namaḥ. Charity: green gram, green cloth. Study, writing and honest speech strengthen Mercury directly.

**Jupiter** — Yellow sapphire (pukhrāj). Colours: yellow, saffron. Day: Thursday. Mantra: Oṁ Bṛhaspataye Namaḥ. Charity: turmeric, yellow cloth, chana dāl, books. Respect for teachers and support of education are the classical Jupiter remedies.

**Venus** — Diamond or white sapphire. Colours: white, pastel, pink. Day: Friday. Mantra: Oṁ Śukrāya Namaḥ. Charity: white cloth, curd, perfume, sugar. Artistic practice and generosity toward women in the family.

**Saturn** — Blue sapphire (nīlam) — the most powerful and the most dangerous stone, never to be worn without careful judgement and a trial period. Colours: dark blue, black. Day: Saturday. Mantra: Oṁ Śanaiścarāya Namaḥ; the Hanumān Chālīsā is also prescribed. Charity: black sesame, iron, mustard oil, black cloth, and feeding labourers and the elderly. Service to the poor is the most effective Saturn remedy of all.

**Rāhu** — Hessonite (gomedh). Colours: smoky grey, brown. Mantra: Oṁ Rāhave Namaḥ, and Durgā worship. Charity: black and white blankets, sesame, mustard oil. Rāhu is best addressed by reducing consumption and ending compulsive patterns.

**Ketu** — Cat's eye (lehsuniyā). Colours: grey, multi-coloured. Mantra: Oṁ Ketave Namaḥ, and Gaṇeśa worship. Charity: blankets, feeding dogs. Meditation and spiritual practice suit Ketu better than any object.

## Gemstone cautions

- A gemstone should never be worn for a planet that rules the 6th, 8th or 12th house from the ascendant, however weak that planet appears.
- Blue sapphire and hessonite are the two stones most often reported to produce immediate adverse effect; both warrant a trial period before permanent wearing.
- A stone must be of adequate weight and genuinely untreated to have the effect claimed for it, which makes the remedy expensive and easy to sell dishonestly.
- Any recommendation should come from a qualified astrologer who has examined the whole chart, not from a table of correspondences.

## Non-gem remedies

These are traditionally regarded as safer and often more effective:

- **Mantra** — repetition of the graha's bīja or stotra, particularly on the graha's weekday.
- **Dāna** — giving the substances of the afflicting planet to those who need them, without display.
- **Fasting** on the planet's weekday.
- **Service** — the remedy that most directly matches the karma being addressed: serving the mother for the Moon, teachers for Jupiter, labourers and the elderly for Saturn.
- **Conduct** — moderating the behaviour the affliction describes. A native with an afflicted Mars who trains their temper is doing more than any coral will do.

## What to tell a person

Frame remedies as supportive practice, never as a transaction that buys off fate. Do not present any remedy as a substitute for medical, legal or financial advice, and do not use a doṣa to create fear and then sell its cure — that pattern is the single most common abuse in commercial astrology.
