# Yogas and Doṣas

A yoga is a specific combination of grahas that produces a result larger than the sum of its parts. A doṣa is an affliction. Both are widely over-claimed; a yoga present in the chart but weak, combust or badly placed will not deliver, and most "rare" yogas are in fact common.

## Rāja yogas — combinations for status

A rāja yoga forms when a lord of a kendra (1, 4, 7, 10) and a lord of a trikona (1, 5, 9) come into relationship — by conjunction, by mutual aspect, or by exchange of signs. The angle supplies the structure and the trine supplies the grace; together they raise the native.

The strength of a rāja yoga depends on where it forms and whether the participating planets are themselves strong. A rāja yoga formed in the 6th, 8th or 12th house, or between debilitated planets, is a promise the chart cannot easily keep.

**Yogakāraka.** When a single planet owns both a kendra and a trikona, it forms a rāja yoga by itself. Saturn is yogakāraka for Taurus and Libra ascendants; Mars for Cancer and Leo; Venus for Capricorn and Aquarius. The daśā of a yogakāraka is typically the making of the life.

## Dhana yogas — combinations for wealth

Wealth is read from the 2nd (accumulation), 11th (income), 5th and 9th (fortune) houses. A dhana yoga forms when the lords of these houses connect — the 2nd lord with the 11th, the 5th lord with the 9th, or the 9th lord with the 2nd.

The 6th, 8th and 12th lords connecting to wealth houses give money that arrives and leaves through unusual channels — debt, inheritance, foreign sources, litigation.

## Pañca Mahāpuruṣa yogas

Five yogas formed when Mars, Mercury, Jupiter, Venus or Saturn occupies its own sign or exaltation **and** sits in a kendra from the ascendant:

- **Ruchaka** (Mars) — courage, command, physical presence, military or engineering distinction.
- **Bhadra** (Mercury) — intellect, eloquence, commercial and scholarly success.
- **Haṁsa** (Jupiter) — wisdom, respect, teaching, moral authority.
- **Mālavya** (Venus) — beauty, comfort, artistic gift, refined enjoyment.
- **Śaśa** (Saturn) — authority over others, endurance, power gained slowly and held long.

These require both conditions. A planet exalted in the 6th house does not form the yoga.

## Gaja Kesari yoga

Jupiter in a kendra from the Moon. Gives intelligence, reputation and the support of good people. Extremely common, and correspondingly modest in effect unless Jupiter is itself strong.

## Nīcabhaṅga Rāja yoga — cancelled debilitation

A debilitated planet's weakness can be cancelled when, for instance, the lord of the sign of debilitation, or the planet that would be exalted in that sign, sits in a kendra from the ascendant or from the Moon. When cancellation occurs, the planet's daśā can invert into a period of unusual rise — success achieved precisely through the area that first appeared to be a weakness.

## Viparīta Rāja yoga

Formed when the lords of the 6th, 8th and 12th houses occupy one another's houses. The principle is that two afflictions cancel: the difficulty destroys the difficulty. It gives sudden rise through the collapse of opponents or circumstances, and is often visible in charts that recover spectacularly from ruin.

## Kemadruma yoga

No planet in the 2nd or 12th from the Moon, and no planet with the Moon. Gives loneliness and a mind without support. It is cancelled if any planet occupies a kendra from the Moon or the ascendant, or if the Moon is aspected by a benefic — which is usually the case, so genuine Kemadruma is rare.

## Maṅgal doṣa (Manglik / Kuja doṣa)

Mars placed in the 1st, 2nd, 4th, 7th, 8th or 12th house — counted from the ascendant, and in stricter practice also from the Moon and from Venus. The affliction is to marriage: Mars in these positions brings friction, delay, or a partner met under difficult circumstances.

Cancellations are numerous and matter more than the doṣa itself:

- Mars in its own sign (Aries, Scorpio) or exalted (Capricorn) is largely neutralised.
- Mars aspected by or conjoined with Jupiter is greatly softened.
- The doṣa is cancelled when both partners carry it.
- Mars in the 2nd in Gemini or Virgo, or in the 12th in Taurus or Libra, is often exempted.
- After roughly the age of thirty the intensity is held to diminish.

Maṅgal doṣa is the most socially damaging over-reading in the whole tradition. It describes a tendency toward friction that needs conscious handling, not an impediment to marriage, and it should never be presented to a person as a verdict.

## Kāla Sarpa doṣa

All seven grahas fall on one side of the Rāhu–Ketu axis. Gives a life of intense, uneven effort — long obstruction followed by sudden release. It is a comparatively modern formulation, absent from the earliest classical texts, and it is cancelled or greatly reduced if even one planet falls outside the axis (Kāla Amṛta) or if strong rāja yogas are present elsewhere.

## Pitṛ doṣa

Affliction to the 9th house or to the Sun, particularly by Rāhu, Ketu or Saturn, read as an ancestral debt. Traditionally addressed through rites for the ancestors rather than through gemstones.

## Reading yogas responsibly

Three cautions:

1. **Check strength before claiming a result.** A yoga formed by a combust, debilitated or duḥsthāna-placed planet is a formality.
2. **Check the daśā.** A yoga delivers during the daśā or antardaśā of its participating planets. A rāja yoga whose planets never receive a daśā in the productive years of the life gives far less than the chart appears to promise.
3. **Count the cancellations.** Every classical doṣa comes with a list of exemptions, and those exemptions apply far more often than the doṣa stands unqualified.
