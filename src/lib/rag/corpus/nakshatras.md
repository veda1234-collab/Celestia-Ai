# The Twenty-Seven Nakṣatras

A nakṣatra is a lunar mansion — one of twenty-seven equal segments of 13°20' each. The nakṣatra of the Moon at birth is the janma nakṣatra, and it determines the starting point of the Vimśottari daśā. Each divides into four pādas of 3°20', which map onto the navāṁśa.

The nakṣatra is often a sharper description of temperament than the sign, because it is narrower and because the Moon carries the mind.

## The nakṣatras, their lords and their character

**Aśvinī** (Ketu) — the horse's head. Swift, healing, pioneering, restless to begin. Aptitude for medicine and for rescue.

**Bharaṇī** (Venus) — the yoni. Bearing and restraint; carries burdens, governs birth and death, creative intensity held under pressure.

**Kṛttikā** (Sun) — the razor and the flame. Sharp, purifying, critical, cuts away what is false. Nourishing and burning in the same gesture.

**Rohiṇī** (Moon) — the chariot. The Moon's favourite; beauty, fertility, growth, magnetism, material comfort, and a streak of possessiveness.

**Mṛgaśirā** (Mars) — the deer's head. Searching, curious, gentle, never quite settled; the perpetual seeker.

**Ārdrā** (Rāhu) — the teardrop. Storm and transformation; sharp intelligence that arrives through disturbance and grief.

**Punarvasu** (Jupiter) — the quiver of arrows. Return, renewal, the capacity to start again after loss. Generous and philosophical.

**Puṣya** (Saturn) — the cow's udder. Widely regarded as the most auspicious nakṣatra: nourishing, dutiful, protective, deeply steady.

**Āśleṣā** (Mercury) — the coiled serpent. Penetrating, hypnotic, strategic; insight that can shade into manipulation.

**Maghā** (Ketu) — the throne. Ancestry, lineage, authority inherited rather than earned; dignity and pride.

**Pūrva Phalgunī** (Venus) — the hammock. Pleasure, romance, rest, creative leisure, generosity.

**Uttara Phalgunī** (Sun) — the bed. Patronage, contract, friendship, reliable partnership and stated obligation.

**Hasta** (Moon) — the hand. Skill, craft, dexterity, cleverness; makes things and gets things.

**Citrā** (Mars) — the bright jewel. Design, architecture, beauty deliberately constructed; brilliance that wants to be seen.

**Svātī** (Rāhu) — the sprout in the wind. Independence, movement, trade, flexibility, self-directed and hard to bind.

**Viśākhā** (Jupiter) — the triumphal arch. Ambition with a fixed goal; determined, competitive, arrives by force of purpose.

**Anurādhā** (Saturn) — the lotus. Friendship, devotion, organisation; succeeds abroad and through loyal alliance.

**Jyeṣṭhā** (Mercury) — the earring. Seniority, protection, hidden power; capable and burdened, often isolated by responsibility.

**Mūla** (Ketu) — the tied roots. Investigation to the root; upheaval, philosophical depth, tears out foundations to find truth.

**Pūrva Āṣāḍhā** (Venus) — the winnowing basket. Invincible conviction, persuasion, purification, unshakeable optimism.

**Uttara Āṣāḍhā** (Sun) — the elephant's tusk. Enduring victory, integrity, leadership that lasts because it is deserved.

**Śravaṇa** (Moon) — the ear. Listening, learning, tradition, scholarship; knowledge received and faithfully transmitted.

**Dhaniṣṭhā** (Mars) — the drum. Rhythm, music, wealth, group achievement, timing and performance.

**Śatabhiṣā** (Rāhu) — the empty circle. Healing, secrecy, solitude, science and mysticism; a hundred physicians and a closed door.

**Pūrva Bhādrapadā** (Jupiter) — the two-faced man. Intensity, asceticism, fire; sees both worlds and is uneasy in each.

**Uttara Bhādrapadā** (Saturn) — the serpent of the deep. Depth, patience, wisdom held quietly, compassion without display.

**Revatī** (Mercury) — the fish and the drum. Nourishment, safe passage, completion; kind, protective and transcendent.

## Pādas

Each nakṣatra divides into four pādas, and the pāda determines the navāṁśa sign. This is why two people born under the same nakṣatra can differ markedly: the pāda places the Moon in an entirely different D9 sign, changing its dignity and its inner direction.

## Using the nakṣatra

- The janma nakṣatra sets the Vimśottari daśā sequence — this is its most consequential use.
- Its lord is a significant planet in the chart, and its daśā periods carry special weight.
- The nakṣatra of the ascendant describes the outward manner; the nakṣatra of the Moon describes the inner life.
- Nakṣatras are used for muhūrta — the election of an auspicious time — and for tārā bala, the daily strength reckoned from the birth star.
- The nakṣatra of a planet refines its expression: the same planet in the same sign behaves differently in Kṛttikā than in Rohiṇī.
