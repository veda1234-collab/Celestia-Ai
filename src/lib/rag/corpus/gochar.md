# Gochar (Transits)

Gochar is the movement of the grahas now, read against the natal chart. In Vedic practice transits are judged primarily **from the natal Moon** (Candra lagna), not from the ascendant — this is one of the sharpest differences from Western technique. Reading from the ascendant as well adds detail, but the Moon is the reference point.

## The benefic houses for each graha from the Moon

A graha gives good results when transiting these houses counted from the natal Moon:

- **Sun:** 3, 6, 10, 11
- **Moon:** 1, 3, 6, 7, 10, 11
- **Mars:** 3, 6, 11
- **Mercury:** 2, 4, 6, 8, 10, 11
- **Jupiter:** 2, 5, 7, 9, 11
- **Venus:** 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 8, 9, 11, 12
- **Saturn:** 3, 6, 11
- **Rāhu and Ketu:** 3, 6, 11

Note how consistently 3, 6 and 11 appear — the upachaya houses, where difficulty converts into achievement. Malefics transiting these houses are genuinely helpful.

## Vedha — obstruction

A transit result does not stand alone. Vedha is the classical rule that a graha occupying a particular partner house cancels the result of a graha transiting its benefic house. The obstruction is mutual: it can just as easily relieve a difficult transit as spoil a good one.

Two exceptions are given: the Sun and Saturn do not obstruct each other, and the Moon and Mercury do not obstruct each other.

In practice, weigh the speed of the obstructing graha. A vedha caused by the transiting Moon lasts about two days and should not be read as cancelling a two-and-a-half-year Saturn transit; a vedha from Jupiter or Saturn holds for months and genuinely matters.

## Sāḍe Sātī

Sāḍe Sātī is Saturn's transit through the three signs surrounding the natal Moon — the 12th, the 1st and the 2nd from it. Since Saturn takes roughly two and a half years per sign, the whole passage runs about seven and a half years, and recurs roughly every thirty.

The three phases, each called a dhaiya:

- **Rising (Saturn in the 12th from the Moon).** Expenditure, disturbed sleep, loss of support, and the quiet removal of things one had relied on. The ground is cleared, often uncomfortably.
- **Peak (Saturn over the natal Moon).** The heaviest phase. The mind itself is under pressure — this is where depression, isolation and health difficulty concentrate. Responsibility increases while enthusiasm falls.
- **Setting (Saturn in the 2nd from the Moon).** Family, speech and finances come under review. The weight lifts gradually, and what survives the passage is durable.

Sāḍe Sātī is not a curse and is badly misrepresented as one. It is a period of enforced maturity. Natives with a strong Saturn — particularly where Saturn is a yogakāraka or occupies its own or exalted sign — frequently experience Sāḍe Sātī as the period in which their career is finally built. What it reliably removes is anything held together by hope rather than by structure.

Because Saturn retrogrades, it may re-enter a sign it has already left, so the true span is measured from the first entry into the 12th to the final exit from the 2nd, which can exceed seven and a half years.

## The dhaiyas — Kaṇṭaka and Aṣṭama Śani

Two other Saturn transits of about two and a half years each are singled out:

- **Kaṇṭaka (or Ardha) Śani** — Saturn in the 4th from the natal Moon. Pressure on home, mother, property and inner peace. Domestic life demands patient maintenance.
- **Aṣṭama Śani** — Saturn in the 8th from the natal Moon. Widely considered the most testing Saturn transit after the peak of Sāḍe Sātī. Guard health, avoid risky commitments and speculative debt, and expect old structures to end.

## Jupiter's transit

Jupiter takes about a year per sign, and its transit is the single most reliable indicator of the year's opportunities. Jupiter transiting the 2, 5, 7, 9 or 11 from the natal Moon supports wealth, children, marriage, fortune and gain respectively. Jupiter transiting the 6, 8 or 12 from the Moon is a year to consolidate rather than expand.

Jupiter's aspect matters as much as its position: it aspects the 5th, 7th and 9th houses from wherever it sits, so a Jupiter that is poorly placed may still be protecting an important house by aspect.

## The nodes

Rāhu and Ketu move retrograde through the zodiac, taking about eighteen months per sign and roughly eighteen years for a full cycle. Their transit over natal points brings sudden, unearned and destabilising events — Rāhu by amplification and craving, Ketu by removal. The return of the nodes to their natal position around age nineteen, thirty-seven and fifty-six often coincides with a decisive reorientation.

## Tārā bala

Tārā bala is a daily measure taken from the transiting Moon's nakshatra counted from the birth nakshatra, cycling through nine tārās: Janma, Sampat, Vipat, Kṣema, Pratyari, Sādhaka, Vadha, Mitra and Ati-Mitra. Sampat, Kṣema, Sādhaka, Mitra and Ati-Mitra are favourable; Janma, Vipat, Pratyari and Vadha are not. It is used for choosing a day rather than for judging a life.

## How to weigh a transit

Slow grahas colour years; fast grahas colour days. Saturn, Jupiter and the nodes set the season, and their transits are what a reading should lead with. Mars and the Sun modulate months. The Moon, Mercury and Venus move too quickly to carry structural meaning — they time events that the slower transits and the running daśā have already made possible.

A transit can only activate what the natal chart promises and the daśā permits. Transits alone predict nothing.
