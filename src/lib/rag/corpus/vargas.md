# Vargas (Divisional Charts)

A varga divides each sign into equal parts and maps those parts onto a fresh twelve-sign chart. Each division magnifies one department of life. The principle is simple and strict: **the rāśi (D1) makes the promise, and the relevant varga decides whether it is kept.**

A planet may look excellent in the birth chart and dissolve in the divisional chart that governs the matter in question. Career is not judged from the tenth house alone but from the tenth house *and* the D10; marriage from the seventh *and* the D9.

## The principal vargas and what they govern

- **D1 Rāśi** — the body, overall life, the physical world.
- **D2 Horā** — wealth and material resources.
- **D3 Drekkāṇa** — siblings, courage, initiative.
- **D4 Chaturthāṁśa** — fortune, fixed property, home, inner happiness.
- **D7 Saptāṁśa** — children and creative continuity.
- **D9 Navāṁśa** — marriage, spouse, dharma, fortune, and the inner strength of every planet.
- **D10 Daśāṁśa** — career, profession, status, worldly achievement.
- **D12 Dvādaśāṁśa** — parents and ancestry.
- **D16 Ṣoḍaśāṁśa** — vehicles, comforts, luxuries.
- **D20 Viṁśāṁśa** — spiritual practice and worship.
- **D24 Siddhāṁśa** — education and acquired learning.
- **D27 Bhāṁśa** — innate strengths, weaknesses, stamina.
- **D30 Triṁśāṁśa** — misfortunes, character, moral tendencies.
- **D40 Khavedāṁśa** — auspicious and inauspicious effects; maternal legacy.
- **D45 Akṣavedāṁśa** — character and conduct; paternal legacy.
- **D60 Ṣaṣṭyāṁśa** — the fine-tuning of every matter; the most granular varga, and in Parāśara's own ranking the most important after the rāśi.

Some divisions in common use — D5, D6, D8 and D11 among them — have no single agreed classical rule. Where they are computed by a documented cyclic scheme rather than an attested Parāśari one, they should be weighed more lightly and never presented as equally authoritative.

## Navāṁśa — the second chart

The D9 is so important that it is effectively read as a second birth chart, and no serious reading omits it. It shows:

- The spouse and the quality of married life.
- The dharma and inner direction of the native.
- The true strength of every planet — a planet strong in D1 but weak in D9 promises more than it delivers, particularly in the second half of life.

## Vargottama

A planet occupying the same sign in the rāśi and in a divisional chart is vargottama. This is a powerful strengthening: a vargottama planet delivers its results consistently and is protected from much of the damage it might otherwise suffer. A vargottama ascendant gives a settled, coherent life direction. Vargottama in the D9 specifically is the classical usage of the term.

## Vimśopaka bala

Vimśopaka bala aggregates a planet's dignity across a set of vargas — usually the sixteen ṣoḍaśavarga — into a single strength score. It answers a question the rāśi cannot: does this planet hold up under magnification?

Read it as a reliability measure. Two planets may sit equally well in the birth chart, but the one with the higher Vimśopaka score will actually deliver its promise, while the other tends to give results that are announced and then withdrawn.

## How to use vargas in a reading

1. Identify the matter and its varga — career to D10, marriage to D9, children to D7, property to D4, education to D24, parents to D12, spiritual life to D20.
2. Find the relevant house and its lord **in the rāśi** and judge the promise.
3. Check the same significator **in the varga**. Is it dignified there? Is it vargottama? Does it fall into a duḥsthāna of the varga?
4. Take the varga lagna seriously — a divisional chart has its own ascendant and its own houses, and planets are judged from it.
5. Weigh the Vimśopaka score before predicting magnitude.

A promise strong in the rāśi and confirmed in the varga is dependable. A promise strong in the rāśi and contradicted in the varga typically manifests as an opportunity that arrives and does not consolidate. A promise weak in the rāśi is not rescued by a strong varga — the varga can only refine what the birth chart already permits.
