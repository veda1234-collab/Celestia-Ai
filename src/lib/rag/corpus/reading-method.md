# How to Read a Chart

A reading is a synthesis, not a list. The commonest failure is to recite isolated facts — "Saturn is in the seventh, Mars is debilitated" — without ever resolving them into a single coherent picture. This is the order in which a chart is actually assembled.

## 1. Establish the frame

Note the ascendant and its lord, the Moon's sign and nakṣatra, and the Sun's sign. The ascendant gives the body and the direction of life, the Moon gives the mind, the Sun gives the soul and the ego. If these three point in different directions, the native will feel divided — and saying so is often the most useful sentence in a reading.

## 2. Work out functional benefics and malefics

Natural character is secondary to lordship. For each ascendant, determine which planets rule trikonas (good), which rule kendras, which rule duḥsthānas, and whether any planet rules both a kendra and a trikona (yogakāraka). This single step changes the meaning of every other placement, and skipping it is why generic readings go wrong.

## 3. Assess strength honestly

For each significant planet, take together: dignity in the rāśi, house placement, combustion, retrogression, aspects received, and Vimśopaka bala across the vargas. A planet that is exalted but combust, or dignified in the rāśi but collapsing across the divisions, cannot deliver what its position appears to promise.

## 4. Judge each matter from three views

For any question, require agreement between the house, its lord, and the natural karaka — then confirm in the relevant varga:

| Matter | House | Kāraka | Varga |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Career | 10th | Sun, Saturn, Mercury | D10 |
| Marriage | 7th | Venus (wife), Jupiter (husband) | D9 |
| Children | 5th | Jupiter | D7 |
| Wealth | 2nd, 11th | Jupiter, Venus | D2 |
| Property | 4th | Mars, Venus | D4 |
| Education | 4th, 5th | Mercury, Jupiter | D24 |
| Health | 1st, 6th | Sun, Saturn | D30 |
| Father | 9th | Sun | D12 |
| Mother | 4th | Moon | D12 |
| Spirituality | 9th, 12th | Jupiter, Ketu | D20 |

One supporting factor is a hint. Two is an indication. Three in agreement is a genuine promise.

## 5. Time it

Find the running mahādaśā, antardaśā and pratyantardaśā. Ask what those lords own, where they sit and what they aspect. A promise in the chart manifests when a planet capable of delivering it holds the daśā. Then overlay the transits, weighting Saturn, Jupiter and the nodes.

The formula: **the chart promises, the daśā permits, the transit triggers.** All three must align for a significant event. This is why nothing happens in some excellent-looking years and a great deal happens in unremarkable ones.

## 6. Synthesise

Reduce everything to two or three governing themes and say them plainly. A person cannot use fourteen observations; they can use one accurate sentence about the shape of their situation and what to do about it.

## Cautions that matter

**Never predict death.** The classical texts discuss longevity at length, and every competent tradition forbids stating it to the native. The same applies to predicting the death of a family member.

**Do not diagnose.** Health indications in a chart point to tendencies and constitutional weakness, never to a specific medical condition. Refer to a doctor.

**Do not give financial or legal directives.** Describe the climate; do not instruct someone to make or avoid a specific investment or case.

**Do not manufacture fear.** Creating alarm about a doṣa and then offering a paid remedy is the defining abuse of commercial astrology. Every classical affliction carries cancellations, and those cancellations apply more often than not.

**Respect free will.** A chart describes tendency and timing, not compulsion. Two people with similar charts live very different lives according to what they do with them. Speak in terms of possibility, pressure and opportunity — never inevitability.

## Tone

The purpose of a reading is to leave the person more capable than they were, not more anxious. When a placement is genuinely difficult, name it honestly, explain what it asks of them, and give something practical to do. Honesty without cruelty; encouragement without flattery.
