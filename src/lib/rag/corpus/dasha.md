# Vimśottari Daśā

The Vimśottari daśā is the principal timing system of Vedic astrology. It divides a notional 120-year lifespan among the nine grahas in a fixed sequence, and the starting point is taken from the Moon's nakshatra at birth.

## The sequence and its lengths

Ketu 7 years, Venus 20, Sun 6, Moon 10, Mars 7, Rāhu 18, Jupiter 16, Saturn 19, Mercury 17 — totalling 120. The order never changes; only the entry point does.

The birth nakshatra determines which lord runs first, and the portion of that nakshatra the Moon has already traversed determines how much of that first mahādaśā has already elapsed at birth. This remainder is called the balance of daśā at birth.

## The levels

Each mahādaśā divides into nine antardaśās in the same sequence, beginning with the mahādaśā lord itself, each proportional to its own daśā length. Each antardaśā divides again into pratyantardaśās, and those into sūkṣmadaśās.

- **Mahādaśā** sets the chapter — the governing theme of years.
- **Antardaśā** sets the year — what actually happens within the chapter.
- **Pratyantardaśā** narrows to months.
- **Sūkṣmadaśā** narrows to weeks and is used for fine timing.

A result promised by the chart manifests when the daśā of a planet capable of delivering it arrives. No daśā can give what the natal chart does not promise; the daśā only opens the door to what is already there.

## Judging a daśā lord

A daśā lord gives according to several factors taken together:

**Ownership from the ascendant.** A planet ruling a trikona (1, 5, 9) gives fortune. Ruling a kendra (4, 7, 10) gives activity and worldly engagement. A planet ruling both a kendra and a trikona is a yogakāraka and its daśā is typically the best period of the life. Ruling a duḥsthāna (6, 8, 12) brings difficulty in proportion.

**Placement.** A planet in a kendra or trikona from the ascendant acts freely. In a duḥsthāna it struggles, and its daśā tends to bring the affairs of that difficult house.

**Dignity.** Exalted or in its own sign, the planet delivers fully. Debilitated or in an enemy sign, it promises and does not pay — unless the debilitation is cancelled.

**Strength across the vargas.** Vimśopaka bala aggregates a planet's dignity across the sixteen divisional charts. A planet that looks strong in the rāśi but collapses across the vargas will not sustain its results.

**The house it occupies and aspects.** The daśā of a planet gives the results of the houses it owns, the house it sits in, and the houses it aspects.

## The antardaśā within the mahādaśā

The antardaśā lord modifies the mahādaśā lord's promise. Judge the relationship between the two:

- If both are benefic for the ascendant and mutually friendly, the period is smooth and productive.
- If the antar lord is in a kendra or trikona from the mahā lord — particularly the 5th, 9th or 11th from it — results are good.
- If the antar lord is in the 6th, 8th or 12th from the mahā lord, the period is obstructed regardless of either planet's individual strength.
- A benefic antardaśā inside a malefic mahādaśā gives temporary relief, not a change of chapter. A malefic antardaśā inside a benefic mahādaśā gives a setback that the wider period absorbs.

## Common patterns

**The yogakāraka daśā** — for Taurus and Libra ascendants Saturn, for Cancer and Leo ascendants Mars, for Capricorn and Aquarius ascendants Venus. These daśās frequently mark the making of the career and the material peak of the life.

**The daśā of the ascendant lord** placed well gives health, direction and self-possession; placed badly it gives a period of drift and physical difficulty.

**The daśā of a debilitated planet** without cancellation gives a period of effort disproportionate to result. If the debilitation is cancelled — by the dispositor being in a kendra from the ascendant or the Moon, or by the exaltation lord of that sign being similarly placed — the same period can invert into unusual success (nīcabhaṅga rāja yoga).

**Rāhu and Ketu daśās** give the results of their dispositor and of any planet conjoined with them. Rāhu's daśā often brings foreign connection, sudden elevation and a period of intense ambition; Ketu's brings separation, spiritual turning, and the quiet dismantling of what no longer serves.

**Marakas.** The daśā or antardaśā of the lords of the 2nd and 7th houses, especially when they are also malefic or badly placed, is when the classical texts locate danger to life — but this is the single most over-read rule in the tradition, and should never be stated to a person as a prediction of death.

## Daśā and transit together

Daśā says when a theme is due; gochar says what the sky is doing about it now. The two are read together. A favourable antardaśā under a hard Saturn transit delivers slowly and at cost, rather than not at all. An excellent transit during a hostile daśā gives an opening that does not last. The strongest results come when the daśā lord is also well placed in transit, and particularly when the transiting Jupiter or Saturn activates the natal house the daśā lord governs.
