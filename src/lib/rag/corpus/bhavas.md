# The Twelve Bhāvas (Houses)

A bhāva is a department of life. This engine uses whole-sign houses: the sign containing the ascendant is the entire first house, the next sign the entire second, and so on. A house is judged by three things together — the planets sitting in it, the planets aspecting it, and the condition of its lord.

## House classification

**Kendras (angles): 1, 4, 7, 10.** The pillars of the chart, governing the visible structure of life. Planets here gain strength to act. Benefics in kendras build; malefics in kendras can be surprisingly constructive, since malefics gain directional strength in angles.

**Trikonas (trines): 1, 5, 9.** The houses of fortune, dharma and grace. These never harm. A planet owning a trikona becomes benefic for that ascendant regardless of its natural character.

**Duḥsthānas (difficult houses): 6, 8, 12.** Houses of debt, rupture and loss. Planets here suffer, and their significations are delayed or damaged — but malefics placed here can paradoxically do well, destroying the difficulty rather than being destroyed by it (the principle of "a malefic in a duḥsthāna gives viparīta results").

**Upachayas (growing houses): 3, 6, 10, 11.** Houses that improve with time and effort. Malefics do genuinely well here; their struggle becomes achievement as life proceeds.

**Marakas (death-inflicting): 2, 7.** Along with their lords, these govern the timing of the end of life.

## First house — Tanu Bhāva

The body, appearance, constitution, temperament, the head, and the overall direction of life. The ascendant is the single most important point in the chart: everything else is measured from it. A strong first house and a strong ascendant lord give vitality, presence and the ability to withstand difficulty elsewhere in the chart.

## Second house — Dhana Bhāva

Accumulated wealth, family of origin, speech, food, the face, and values in the literal sense of what one holds worth having. The second is also a maraka. A well-disposed second house gives savings, a pleasant voice and a supportive family; afflicted, it gives harsh speech, family friction and money that does not stay.

## Third house — Sahaja Bhāva

Younger siblings, courage, initiative, short journeys, hands and arms, communication, and self-effort. An upachaya house: malefics here give the drive to push through obstacles. A strong third gives enterprise and the willingness to act without permission.

## Fourth house — Sukha Bhāva

Mother, home, land and fixed property, vehicles, education in its formative sense, the heart, and inner contentment. The fourth is the seat of happiness — a chart may be outwardly successful and still restless if the fourth is afflicted. The Moon and Mars are the natural karakas here.

## Fifth house — Putra Bhāva

Children, intelligence, creativity, speculation, romance, mantra and pūrva puṇya — the merit carried from previous action. A trikona, and therefore never harmful in itself. Jupiter is the karaka for children. A strong fifth gives quick understanding, creative issue and good fortune that seems unearned.

## Sixth house — Ari Bhāva

Enemies, disease, debt, litigation, service and daily work, maternal uncle, and the digestive tract. A duḥsthāna but also an upachaya: the sixth rewards those who compete. Malefics here defeat enemies and clear debts. A strong sixth lord in a bad position, however, can make the native their own obstacle.

## Seventh house — Kalatra Bhāva

Marriage, the spouse, business partnership, open enemies, trade, and the reproductive organs. Directly opposite the ascendant, it governs everything the self meets as an equal. Venus is the karaka for the wife, Jupiter for the husband in a female chart. The seventh is also a maraka.

## Eighth house — Āyur Bhāva

Longevity, death, sudden and unexpected events, inheritance, the spouse's wealth, occult knowledge, chronic illness, and transformation. The most difficult house, but the seat of research and of every kind of hidden knowledge. Saturn is the karaka for longevity. A strong eighth can give unexpected gain and deep insight; afflicted, it gives accidents, scandal and prolonged illness.

## Ninth house — Dharma Bhāva

Fortune, father, guru, higher learning, long journeys, pilgrimage, philosophy, and righteousness. The most auspicious house in the chart. Jupiter and the Sun are its karakas. A strong ninth gives protection through life, good teachers, and the sense that one is on the right road.

## Tenth house — Karma Bhāva

Career, profession, status, authority, action in the world, and the father in some schools. The highest point of the chart and the most visible. Planets here are seen by everyone. The tenth lord's condition, the tenth house of the D10 chart, and the placement of the Sun, Saturn and Mercury all bear on profession.

## Eleventh house — Lābha Bhāva

Gains, income, elder siblings, friends, networks, and the fulfilment of desires. An upachaya, and the house of realised profit as opposed to the second house of stored wealth. A strong eleventh gives ample income and useful friends; it is also the house of insatiability, since desires fulfilled generate more desire.

## Twelfth house — Vyaya Bhāva

Loss, expenditure, foreign lands, isolation, hospitals and prisons, the bed, sleep, and moksha. What is spent here is gone — but a strong twelfth spends well: on charity, on spiritual practice, on travel that changes the person. Planets in the twelfth lose worldly force and gain inward reach. Venus is said to be excellent in the twelfth for the enjoyment of the bed and for liberation.

## Reading a house

To judge any matter, take three views and require agreement between them:

1. The house itself — its occupants and the aspects falling on it.
2. Its lord — where that lord sits, its dignity, and whether it is strong enough to deliver.
3. The natural karaka of the matter — Jupiter for children, Venus for marriage, the Sun for father, the Moon for mother, Saturn for longevity.

A promise made by only one of these three is weak. When all three agree, the result is reliable and the daśā of the relevant planet will bring it.
